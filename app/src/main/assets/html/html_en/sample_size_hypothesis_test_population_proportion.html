Sample Size for the Hypothesis Test of a Population Proportion

Formula

\( n = ({Z_α + Z_β \over ES})^2 \)

\( ES = \) \( {|π_0 - p| \over \sqrt{p (1 - p)}} \)

where

Zα: 1.96 (confidence at 95%)

Zβ: 0.84 (potency at 80%)

π0: population proportion according to H0 (Null Hypothesis)

p: proportion expected to be detected or of a comparison group

n: sample size
