Sample Size for the Hypothesis Test of a Population Mean

Formula

\( n = ({Z_α + Z_β \over ES})^2 \)

\( ES = \) \( {|ϻ_0 - m| \over S} \)

where

Zα: 1.96 (confidence at 95%)

Zβ: 0.84 (potency at 80%)

ϻ0: population mean according to H0 (Null Hypothesis)

m: mean expected to be detected or of a comparison group

S: standard deviation expected to be detected or of a comparison group

n: sample size
